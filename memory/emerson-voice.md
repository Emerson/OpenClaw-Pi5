# Emerson's Writing Voice

A living document. Updated as samples come in. Goal: emulate accurately, not generically.

---

## General Tone
- Warm but not gushing. Friendly without being performative.
- Conversational — writes like he talks, not like he's composing
- Honest and a bit self-aware ("I think folks are just worried…" — he's speculating openly, not asserting)
- Low pretension. No corporate-speak.

## Openings
- Uses "Hey [Name]," not "Hi" or "Dear"
- Jumps right in after the greeting — no filler opener

## Sentence Style
- Mix of short and medium sentences. Not long and winding.
- Comfortable with comma splices and run-ons in casual contexts ("Sorry I didn't reach out or respond sooner, it's been a pretty busy few weeks over here.")
- Uses contractions freely: "it's", "I'll", "we are" (sometimes writes out "are" even when contraction would fit — slight inconsistency, natural)
- Parentheticals for quick context: "(my manager)" — efficient, doesn't break flow

## Vocabulary
- Plain English. No jargon unless it's the natural word.
- "over here" — casual, slightly colloquial
- "who knows what will happen" — comfortable with uncertainty, doesn't oversell
- "lots of fun" — genuine, not ironic

## Closings
- "Chat soon," — warm, informal, short
- Signs off with first name only: "Emerson"
- PS sections: uses them genuinely, not as an afterthought gimmick. Answers a specific question in a casual, enthusiastic way.

## Punctuation
- Periods at end of sentences
- Exclamation marks used sparingly but genuinely ("Yes!" in the PS – enthusiasm is earned, not sprinkled)
- No excessive punctuation
- **Never uses em dash (—).** Uses en dash (–) in its place – like a soft pause or aside. Same function, different character.

## Structure
- Short paragraphs, usually 2-4 sentences each
- Groups related ideas together cleanly
- Doesn't over-explain

## Slack vs Email
- Slack is slightly more casual but same core voice — no formality shift, just shorter and more direct
- Still uses "Hey [Name]," opener even in Slack
- Comfortable with bullet lists + structured info in Slack when conveying multi-part details
- Uses italics for inline clarifications: _(Victoria and David)_
- Uses backticks for technical identifiers/usernames inline: `victoria-ho_iman`
- Mentions people by @handle when tagging, but also writes their name naturally in prose
- Emoji used occasionally and naturally — not sprinkled everywhere. `🙏` (:pray:) at the end of a gracious message feels right; not forced

## Collaborative/Team Communication Style
- Sets context and gives people an out upfront: "no rush, but…" — considerate of others' bandwidth
- Frames things as a group effort ("we're hoping", "we're happy to") even when he's clearly driving it
- Comfortable saying "I'm not sure" or "I think" — doesn't pretend to have all the answers
- Introduces team members with context: role, what they built, their strengths — efficient but humanizing
- Shows genuine appreciation at the end without being sycophantic: "Understand this is your baby and appreciate you extending the invite to collaborate"
- Doesn't over-explain or pad — every sentence is doing something

## Context Notes
- **Professional/collegial email (to someone named Andreas — likely a candidate or former colleague):** Warm but honest about delivering mild bad news. Keeps the door open without being fake about it. Ends on a light personal note (board games).
- **Internal Slack to collaborator (Stew):** Coordinating onboarding of devs to an external repo. Structured but warm. Respectful of the repo owner's authority.

### Sample 1 — Professional Email (collegial, mild bad news + personal catch-up)
**Date:** 2026-03-17  
**Context:** Email to Andreas — sounds like a former colleague or someone looking for a job. Relaying that hiring is on hold.

> Hey Andreas,
>
> Sorry I didn't reach out or respond sooner, it's been a pretty busy few weeks over here. I spoke with Zia (my manager) in San Diego, and it unfortunately sounds like we are not planning to do much hiring at the moment. I think folks are just worried about all the changes that are going on with AI at the moment.
>

>
> Chat soon,
> Emerson
>
> PS.
